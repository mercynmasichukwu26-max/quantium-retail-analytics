Quantium Virtual Internship: Retail Strategy & Analytics Task 1

Project Overview
Completed Task 1 of the Quantium Data Analytics Virtual Internship on Forage.
Analysed chip purchasing behaviour across customer segments to provide 
strategic recommendations to the Category Manager.

Tools Used
- Python (Jupyter Notebook)
- Pandas: data cleaning and analysis
- Matplotlib & Seaborn: data visualisation

What Was Done
1. Loaded and explored transaction data (264,836 rows) and customer data
2. Cleaned the data:
   - Removed 18,094 salsa product transactions
   - Removed outlier customer who bought 200 packets twice
   - Fixed DATE column from integer to proper date format
   - Standardised 8 inconsistent brand names
   - Extracted PACK_SIZE and BRAND from product names
3. Merged transaction and customer data together
4. Analysed sales by customer segment
5. Created 5 charts to visualise findings

Key Findings
- Budget Older Families spend the most on chips overall
- Mainstream Young Singles/Couples are the largest customer group
- Mainstream Young Singles/Couples pay the highest price per unit
- Kettle is the most popular brand for the target segment
- 175g is the most popular pack size

Recommendations
- Target Mainstream Young Singles/Couples — largest group and highest price per unit
- Stock more Kettle 175g chips for this segment
- Retain Budget Older Families — highest total spend
- Review shelf space allocated to Premium chip products
